# TikTok Surprise Set Streamer Guide

Updated: 2026-05-29

Audience: Degen streamers running official TikTok Surprise Sets.

Use this as the live-room playbook. The goal is simple: make the set feel fast, loud, transparent, and hard to ignore. Use TikTok's official Surprise Set, auction, and reveal tools only.

## The Job

You are not just running auctions. You are driving attention between two places:

- The wall: makes people want in.
- The bottom card: lets viewers verify what is in the set, what is left, and what already sold.

If the room feels slow, it is usually because the host stopped pointing people at one of those two things.

![Visual setup diagram](/static/team-documents/diagrams/visual-setup.svg)

## How The Set Should Be Built

Start with one clean Pokemon sealed-product set. Do not get clever until this basic version is working.

Suggested first pool:

- Total pool: 400 items.
- Base: 300 to 325 items. Use a real sealed JP/CN/KR booster pack or another recognizable sealed pack.
- Mid: 40 to 60 items. Use premium bags, build-and-battle boxes, booster bundles, tins, poster collections, pin collections, or branded sealed boxes.
- Hits: 20 to 35 items. Use ETBs, PC ETBs, UPCs, premium collections, booster boxes, and current hot Pokemon products.

The base item can be cheap. It cannot feel fake. A real sealed pack is easier to explain, easier to ship, and easier to defend when someone lands it at a high bid.

The winning competitor format is not mystery singles. It is a sealed wall with a huge base stack.

The play is obvious: sell repeated shots at a real wall, with one boring/common result and enough visible heat to keep people bidding.

## Room Setup

The stream should look loaded before anyone hears the host.

Set the table like this:

- Top hits in the center and upper third of the frame.
- Base packs or bags stacked in quantity so the set looks real.
- Degen branding visible, but not blocking product.
- Host off-center enough to point at hits without covering them.
- Phone/camera locked. No wandering frame.
- A second screen or printed card with the current count, top hits, and host lines.

Do not put the best hit on the far edge. Do not hide the base. Do not let the chair become the main character.

## Bottom Card Callout

The host cannot open the viewer's bottom panel for them. Your job is to keep telling viewers to tap the Surprise Set card on their own screen.

![Bottom panel callout diagram](/static/team-documents/diagrams/bottom-panel.svg)

What to say:

```text
Tap the card at the bottom.
Available tells you what is still live.
Sold tells you what people already hit.
The count is right there. We are at [count] out of [total].
```

When someone asks what is in the set, do not freestyle for two minutes. Tell them to tap the card, name the top hits, and keep the auction moving.

## The Auction Loop

Run the same loop every time. Boring operations, loud delivery.

![Auction loop diagram](/static/team-documents/diagrams/auction-loop.svg)

1. Start the auction.
2. Point at the wall.
3. Tell chat to tap the card on their screen.
4. Call current bid and remaining count.
5. Push the bid in the last 15 seconds.
6. Reveal the result.
7. Name the item and reset immediately.

No dead air between steps. If a result is weak, say what it is and move. Do not apologize the room into silence.

## Host Voice

This should sound like a live seller, not a training video.

Use short lines:

- "Dollar start. We are live."
- "Tap the card on your screen. Look at available."
- "Top hits are still up."
- "This bid is light."
- "Chat, this is too cheap."
- "Do not let this one sleep."
- "Good luck. Banger incoming."
- "Sold tab should show it."
- "Next one is already queued."

Say the same things more often than feels natural. Viewers enter late. People miss the first explanation. Repetition sells.

## Opening Talk Track

Use this when a Surprise Set block starts:

```text
Alright chat, Surprise Set is live.
Official TikTok flow. Dollar start.

Top hits are on screen. Tap the card at the bottom before you bid.
Available shows what is still in. Sold shows what already hit.

We are moving fast. If you like the wall, get in.
```

If the room is cold:

```text
Do not wait for me to explain it five times.
Tap the bottom card. The set is public. The wall is right there.
Dollar start is live.
```

## Standard Auction Talk Track

Use this every auction:

```text
We are live. Dollar start.
[count] out of [total] left.

Big ones still up: [hit 1], [hit 2], [hit 3].
Tap available. Tap sold. You can see it yourself.

Current bid is [price]. That is light for what is still on the board.
```

If the bid stalls:

```text
Chat, this is too low.
The available tab tells you exactly what is still live.
[hit] is still live. [hit] is still live.
Do not wake up after it hits.
```

If two people are fighting:

```text
[Buyer 1] and [Buyer 2] are on it.
This is exactly where the set gets fun.
Timer is moving. Best bid takes the reveal.
```

## Last 15 Seconds

At 15 seconds:

```text
Fifteen seconds.
[Buyer] is winning at [price].
These bids are light. Top hits are still live.
```

At 8 seconds:

```text
Eight seconds. Tap the card if you need to see it.
This one is going.
```

At 5 seconds:

```text
Five seconds. Good luck.
Banger incoming. Let's see it.
```

## Winner And Reveal

When someone wins:

```text
[Buyer], you got it.
Winning bid was [price].
Everybody watch the reveal.
```

If it is a good hit:

```text
There it is.
[Item] just hit.
That is why the wall is on screen.
Sold tab should show it. Next one is going up.
```

If it is the base item:

```text
That one is [base item].
Common outcome, listed in the set.
Tap available before every bid. TikTok lists the pool.
Next one is live.
```

Do not overreact to every base item. A clean, fast base reveal is better than acting guilty.

## Near-End Pressure

The end of the set is where you should push hardest. The remaining count makes the pitch for you.

When under 15 percent remains:

```text
This is the part people wait for.
Only [count] left out of [total].
[hit 1] and [hit 2] are still sitting there.
These bids should not be this low.
```

When under 5 percent remains and a big hit is still live:

```text
Chat, look at the count.
[count] left. [hit] is still in.
This is not the time to watch quietly.
```

If the room needs a stronger shove:

```text
I cannot bid for you.
I can show you the wall. You can open the available tab.
This is the spot.
```

## What To Say When Chat Pushes Back

If someone says, "I thought this was one dollar":

```text
It starts at one dollar. The final price is the winning bid. TikTok shows the current bid on the card.
```

If someone asks, "What can I win?":

```text
Tap the Surprise Set card. Available shows what is still in. Sold shows what already came out.
```

If someone complains after a base item:

```text
That item is in the listed pool. The available tab is public before every bid.
```

If someone asks whether it is official:

```text
Yes. Official TikTok Surprise Set, official TikTok auction, official TikTok reveal.
```

## Lines That Are Fine

Use these when the panel supports the hype:

- "Insane odds right now."
- "These bids are too low."
- "This is light."
- "The wall is still loaded."
- "Top hits are live."
- "Do not sleep."
- "This is the spot."
- "Check sold. Stuff is hitting."
- "The count is getting dangerous."
- "Best bid gets the reveal."

## Lines To Avoid

Do not say these:

- "Guaranteed value."
- "You cannot lose."
- "You are making money here."
- "Everybody gets a hit."
- "This is free money."
- "Floor is X, ceiling is Y" unless TikTok's official listing and policy support that language.
- "Bid more because I need it."

We can be aggressive. We cannot sound like we are lying.

## Streamer Checklist

Before going live:

- The set is built in TikTok's official Surprise Set tool.
- Every outcome and quantity is correct.
- The base item is clear.
- Top hits are on camera.
- The host knows the top three remaining hits.
- The host has the talk track nearby.
- The bottom card is visible on mobile.

During the stream:

- Say the current bid.
- Say the remaining count.
- Name the top hits.
- Tell viewers to tap the card, then check available and sold.
- Push in the final 15 seconds.
- Reveal cleanly.
- Reset immediately.

After the stream:

- Record average winning bid.
- Record how many base outcomes sold.
- Record which hits sold.
- Note complaint patterns.
- Decide whether to reload, rebuild, or retire the set.

## Manager Coaching Notes

If a streamer sounds flat, coach one thing at a time.

- Too quiet: make them call the bid every 10 seconds.
- Too vague: make them name three remaining hits.
- Too slow: make them start the next auction before explaining the last one for too long.
- Too apologetic: make them say the base item cleanly and move on.
- Too salesy without proof: make them say "tap available" before "insane odds."

The best host line is still:

```text
Dollar start. Top hits are on screen. Tap the card on your screen, check available, check sold.
Current bid is too low for what is still live. Good luck. Banger incoming.
```

That is the whole machine: wall, panel, pressure, reveal, reset.
